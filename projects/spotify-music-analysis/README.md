# Spotify Music Analysis

This project presents exploratory analysis of five Spotify datasets covering tracks, artists, genres, years, and track-to-genre mappings. The supplied presentation is included in `docs/spotify_music_analysis.pdf`.

## Tools

Python, Pandas, Seaborn, Matplotlib, Google Colab, and Canva.

## Project objectives

- Understand music and listening trends across years, genres, and artists.
- Explore audio features including danceability, energy, tempo, loudness, and valence.
- Examine factors related to track popularity.
- Compare artist and genre popularity.
- Communicate findings with charts, scatter plots, line charts, and a correlation heatmap.

## Highlights from the presentation

- The dataset covers 170,653 tracks from 1921 to 2020.
- Most tracks sit in the low-to-medium popularity range; highly popular tracks are rare.
- Energy and danceability trend upward over time, particularly after the 1960s.
- Energy and loudness have a strong positive relationship, while acousticness is negatively associated with them.
- Tempo alone does not strongly predict popularity.
